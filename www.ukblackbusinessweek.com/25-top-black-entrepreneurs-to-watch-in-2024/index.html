HSBC UK & UK Black Business Show: Meet 25 Top Black Entrepreneurs to Watch in 2024

Building upon the remarkable achievements of the prestigious UK Black Business Show X HSBC UK 25 Black Entrepreneurs to watch list in 2021, 2022 & 2023 we are delighted to present you with a fresh compilation of sensational Black entrepreneurs who are changing the landscape in the UK.

Black entrepreneurs continue to face significant challenges, many of which remain unresolved. Race should never be a barrier to success. HSBC UK x UK Black Business Shows 25 Black Entrepreneurs to Watch List serves as an essential source of inspiration. It highlights the outstanding achievements of Black entrepreneurs, offering role models for emerging talents.

Allies have a pivotal role in creating equitable opportunities. By supporting Black entrepreneurs, allies can help level the playing field and open doors that should be accessible to everyone. This support is crucial in ensuring that deserving individuals are given the chance to succeed and make their mark in their respective industries.

This list not only aims to inspire but also to remind us of all that true progress is made through collaboration and mutual support. By recognising and celebrating these trailblazers, we move closer to a world where success is determined by talent and effort, not by race.

Celebrating its eighth year, the annual UK Black Business Show aligns with Black History Month, creating an inspirational hub for Black business owners and professionals from various industries. This dynamic event is steadfast in its mission to uplift underrepresented Black entrepreneurs and professionals. It has now become a pivotal part of UK Black Business Weeka ground-breaking event focused on empowering Black entrepreneurs, professionals, and allies within the community.

As a company, we wholeheartedly embrace the spirit of progress, collaboration, and celebration, highlighting the outstanding achievements of these pioneering Black entrepreneurs.

Founder and CEO of the Show & Week, Dr. Raphael Sofoluke spoke on the importance of championing Black entrepreneurs and the need to highlight Black role models. He says.

”We are thrilled to highlight 25 outstanding Black entrepreneurs once again, marking the fourth consecutive year of this celebration in collaboration with our valued partner, HSBC. This milestone signifies our collective achievement of honouring 100 Black entrepreneurs through this prestigious list, reflecting a remarkable journey of recognition and support.
This year's selection of entrepreneurs exemplifies exceptional quality. They truly deserve to be celebrated, praised, and amplified. It is our distinct pleasure to present to you another extraordinary list of leaders.

Dr. Raphael Sofoluke, Founder and CEO of UK Black Business Show & UK Black Business Week.

@raphaelsofoluke

Peter McIntyre, Head of Business Banking, HSBC UK, said:

”Support for Black entrepreneurs is crucial to ensuring we have the very best talent at the forefront of the UKs industries. Each of the Black leaders on the list has their own remarkable story and we hope that it can inspire others to chase their business ambitions.

Top 25 Black Entrepreneurs:

Picture courtesyof A&O Studios

Ahmed Faid & Nii Lartey

CEO & COO of Dose of Society

Nii Lartey and Ahmed Faid are the Co-founders of Dose of Society, a digital media company invested by Snapchat. With over 6 million followers and 9 billion views, Dose of Society is a trailblazer in authentic storytelling, partnering with brands such as Valentino, Netflix, and Spotify. Recently honoured as Forbes 30 under 30 recipients, Nii and Ahmed continue to inspire change through heartfelt conversations and impactful content.

@doseofsociety

Alison Burton & Natalie Duvall

Project Director & Creative Director of March Muses

March Muses, founded by two Croydon entrepreneurial mums, Alison Burton and Natalie Duvall, are the UK leaders in inclusive Christmas decorations.

Motivated by their daughters’ inability to see themselves reflected in festive ornaments, they launched a range featuring Black angels and Black Santa baubles. Each piece is meticulously handmade, with careful attention to skin tone, hair design, and body shape.

Their achievements include listings in Selfridges and Liberty London and securing 50,000 from investors Peter Jones and Deborah Meaden on BBC’s Dragons Den in 2022.

In 2023, they signed a history making 5 year deal with supermarket giants Tesco to create and develop diverse Christmas decorations and year-round gifting products, including Mothers Day, taking their brand to the masses.

March Muses’ mission is to celebrate diversity by diversifying celebrations, ensuring everyone feels represented during the festive season and beyond.

@marchmuses

Dr. Byron Cole

Global Business Strategist

Dr. Byron Cole is a Forbes featured, multi award winning global business strategist. He specialises in helping businesses grow to 7 figures while guiding owners to create passive and semi passive income streams through writing books, creating courses and becoming professional paid public speakers.

As a Sunday Times bestselling author of three books with two of the largest publishers in the world, Byron brings a wealth of experience. From growing up on a notorious council estate to running multiple 6 and 7-figure businesses, Byron has helped thousands achieve financial freedom.

Byron’s philanthropic efforts and passion for sharing knowledge contributed to earning his Doctorate from the University of Greenwich.

@mrbselfmade

Candice Braithwaite

Author, Journalist, Podcaster, Speaker and Presenter

Candice Brathwaite is a prominent British author and advocate who has made a significant impact with her work on motherhood and diversity. Widely recognised for her critically-acclaimed Sunday Times Best-Selling book, I Am Not Your Baby Mother, Candice passionately addresses the unique experiences of Black British mothers. Her latest book, Manifest(o) is due to be released on 5th September and she sets off on her biggest UK tour to date this September.

Her advocacy extends beyond literature. As a skilled public speaker, she actively engages in discussions and panels on race, parenting, and societal inclusivity. Candice continues to contribute to important dialogues, challenge norms, and promote positive change in the realm of diversity and representation.

Her holistic and exuberant online content has gained her a dedicated audience of 299k+ Instagram followers and 374k+ on TikTok.

Candice is a co-host of the smash-hit podcast Closet Confessions, which amassed over 3 million downloads and several sell-out shows in its first year of production.

@candicebrathwaite

Courtney Daniella Boateng and Renee Kapuku

Founders and Co-hosts of To My Sisters

Courtney Daniella Boateng and Renee Kapuku are two women on a mission – to see women win. The two women are the co-founders and co-hosts of To My Sisters, a leading community-powered womens organisation and fastest-growing sisterhood. Starting as a hit podcast in 2020, TMS has since blossomed into a truly global community of young, enterprising and self-aware women with a mandate to change their lives. Through live events, retreats, resources, productions, and more, TMS has one mission – to inspire 1 million women across the globe to grow and glow together.

@tomysisterhood

Danielle 'Ebonix' Udogaranya

Multi Award-Winning Games Consultant, content creator and multidisciplinary 3D artist

Danielle Udogaranya fondly known as Ebonix is a multi Award-Winning Games Consultant, content creator and multidisciplinary 3D artist. She is Founder of Evolotu, a consultancy focused on evolving cultural visibility within Games and Media, co-founder of Black Twitch UK which supports and empowers Black streamers in the UK and the first Black British woman to be a Twitch Partner and Ambassador.

Danielle taught herself to create video game assets, with a focus on black hairstyles due to lack of meaningful representation in games. Her work resonated with the gaming community, leading her to work directly with Electronic Arts on the addition of over 100 skin tones and black hairstyles to The Sims 4. She has become a celebrated advocate for diversity and representation in gaming, having been featured on Apple, BBC, The Guardian, and many more and consulted on award-winning gaming campaigns, including Play In Color, a collaboration with Dark & Lovely, which won 6 Telly Awards.

Danielles mission is to advocate, create space and generate greater, positive representation for Black people and POC within gaming.

@ebonixsims

David Olusegun

Co-Founder of Creative Control Ventures

David Olusegun is Co-Founder of CCV, a venture builder revolutionising the CPG industry by partnering with celebrities to develop innovative global brands. The first brand, Nala’s Baby, a natural skincare line for infants co-founded with Krept and Sasha Ellese, sold out within ten minutes of its launch. CCV’s second venture, SYPS, co-founded with rapper Aitch, offers flavoured sparkling water with zero sugar and calories, receiving acclaim for quality and taste.

With over a decade of experience in entertainment and media, David is known for his visionary strategy, P&L expertise, and creative communication. He has worked with Warner Music, MTV, Nickelodeon, Nike, and Sony Pictures. Honoured with multiple accolades, David is recognised as one of the Top 100 African Business Leaders in the UK, featured in Music Week’s 30 Under 30 list, and on Powerful Media’s Future Leader list, cementing his reputation as an industry trailblazer.

@dolusegun

Emmanuel Eribo

Co-Founder and CEO of LCI

After graduating from Bath University with a BSC in Psychology Emmanuel spent 8 years working in the city on multiple trading floors at HSBC, Morgan Stanley & CitiGroup.

In 2008, Emmanuel Co-founded the brand, Butterfly Twists, the notorious ballet shoe brand fronted today by the likes of Catherine Zeta-Jones. As CEO Emmanuel guided the business to global success, selling over 2.7million pairs in 66 countries around the world, opening 30+ monoband stores and achieving tens of millions of pounds in sales. The rapid expansion of Butterfly Twists spring-boarded Emmanuel from a kitchen table in Shepherds Bush into the world of mass manufacturing an industry which they knew little about. Each journey to the Far East forced Emmanuel to confront the realities of overproduction and environmental damage caused by the fashion industry. Standing in a warehouse the size of a football stadium with 70,000 pairs of shoes in front of him, the idea for Emmanuels next venture was planted. Developing a new, more progressive shoe brand that would champion change in the industry and operate with 100% sustainability LCI was born.

@lociwear

George Mpanga (George the Poet)

Spoken Word Artist and Podcaster

George the Poet is a London-born spoken word performer of Ugandan heritage. His innovative brand of musical poetry has won him critical acclaim both as a recording artist and social commentator and seen his work broadcast to billions of people worldwide. In 2019, his audio offering Have You Heard Georges Podcast? won a prestigious Peabody Award and 5 Gold British Podcast Awards, including the highly-coveted Podcast of the Year. More accolades followed in the years to come, including an NME Award, two Gold ARIAS, three New York Festival Awards and more. In 2021 The University of London presented George with an honorary doctorate in literature. George is now embarking on a PhD at UCL’S Institute for Innovation and Public Purpose, under the supervision of the institute’s founder, Mariana Mazzucato, and Reader in Educational Leadership At UCL’s Institute Of Education, Dr. Karen Edge. His research focuses on the socio-economic potential of black music.

@georgethepoet

Hayley Mulenda

International Speaker, Author, Consultant, Change-Agent

Hayley Mulenda Record is a Multi-Award Winning International Speaker who works with some of the world’s foremost organizations and institutions. Her story has touched millions, as she shares her journey of turning pain into purpose and the power of authenticity. She has collaborated with notable entities like Microsoft, the University of Cambridge, Google, JP Morgan, NAS Dubai, IBM, and many more. Hayley has delivered keynote speeches on some of the world’s most prestigious stages, including the Houses of Parliament, Oslo Spektrum, and Wembley Arena.

Recognised as one of the most influential voices of her generation, Hayley serves on the board of Wagamama and she sits as a school governor for her former secondary school. Hayley trains hundreds of women across Europe, the USA, Africa, and the Middle East, helping female leaders with their public speaking, their storytelling & confidence.

Hayley began her speaking career at the age of 15, driven by a desire to help others find their voice and bring hope to those without it. Her impact has been acknowledged with awards such as The Diana Award, Rare Rising Stars, and the Best of Congo Awards. Her voice and story will leave you inspired and empowered to overcome any challenges you may face.

@hayleymulenda

Henrie Kwushue

Presenter and DJ

Hailing from Southeast London, Henrie Kwushue is a DJ, presenter and broadcaster best known for her work on Spotifys Who We Be Talks podcast, KISS Fresh evening show, as well as her incredible contribution to the popular No Signal Radio station.

A polymath, Henrie also runs her own production company, HTK Productions, and has produced two shows, the acclaimed Is Your Area Changing – a docu-series focused on the topic of gentrification in London neighborhoods, and Untold Stories a web series about personal stories where guests discuss what has made them who they are today.

Henrie’s ascent into the public eye has been rapid; and she has carefully, and creatively, carved out her own unique lane in British DJing and presenting. She has already graced the stages and dancefloors for the likes of Puma, Adidas, Nike, Nandos, the NFL, and has been covered by media titles including The Guardian, Dazed, Complex UK, gal-dem and Glamour Magazine.

@henrieviii

Ibrahim Kamara

Co-founder of GUAP

Ibrahim is a digital creative entrepreneur and co-founder of GUAP, which has grown from the worlds first video magazine to becoming a multi platform youth media brand, covering all aspects of creativity and culture. GUAP produces content (both in print & online), can boast an events arm, a podcast studio, a youth creative agency and is responsible for some of the most prolific moments in the culture calendar.

His accolades span across being an award-winning entrepreneur, speaker and investor. Headline moments included being one of the 2023 of the Forbes30under30 Faces of Europe for the Media and Marketing category, being honoured as a Digital Pioneer by The Mayor Of London for his work in entrepreneurship in digital media, named as 1 of the top 100 most influential BAME leaders in the UK Tech Sector published in the Financial Times, delivering a TedX talk on How To Take Control of Your Life, delivering a talk on Creativity for META at Soho Farmhouse to the countrys leading advertisers and industry leaders

@ibrahimkamara_

Joanna Jarjue

Founder, Political Commentator and Board Member

Joanna was a BBC Apprentice Final Five candidate in 2017. Her background and experience are in marketing as an Agency Development Manager on behalf of Google, an independent digital marketing consultant and currently as Co-Founder of digital marketing agency ‘So 93’ in Leeds. Along with her team, she specialises in helping businesses grow through Google Ads, Social Media Ads and Video Animation. She remains a weekly current affairs commentator for national television and radio broadcasters – With credits including BBC News ‘The Papers’, Sky Newspaper Review, and ITV’s Good Morning Britain and This Morning. With a passion for civil society as well as business, Joanna is a board member for Impact Education Multi-Academy Trust, English Speaking Board International and is a Goodwill Ambassador for water poverty charity Pump Aid.

Joanna is currently developing the women’s workwear venture originally pitched on The Apprentice, but this time powered by tech. The platform will curate workwear pieces from familiar brands for a one-stop shop to easily find the best workwear to suit most industries and professional occasions, from business casual to business formal.

@joanna_jarjue

Kameese Davis

Founder of Nylahs

Kameese Davis is a trailblazing entrepreneur with a relentless commitment to revolutionising beauty standards and hair care through her company, Nylah’s. Fuelled by a passion for diversity and a desire to provide clean, plant-based alternatives for textured hair, Kameese founded Nylah’s, a hair care product line that has become a beacon of inclusivity and sustainability.

@i_am_kameese

Kazeem Alli- Balogun

Property Developer & Founder of Property By Kazy

Kazeem Alli-Balogun is a seasoned property developer and founder of the PBK Podcast and PBK Community, with over 10 years of experience buying, selling and renovating property.

After sharing his journey on social media in 2020 he has now amassed a following of over 70,000 people across social media platforms and has become a recognised voice in the property world, particularly among ethnic minorities and aspiring property investors.

His podcast, the PBK Podcast, serves as a vital platform for discussing the challenges and opportunities within the property market, aiming to educate and inspire a new generation of property enthusiasts.

Kazeem’s commitment to diversity and inclusion is further reflected through his leadership of the PBK Community which has become a cornerstone for those seeking guidance and encouragement through their investment journey.

@propertybykaz

Kenny Imafidon

Entrepreneur, Author, Social Commentator

Kenny Imafidon is an entrepreneur, author, and social commentator. He is the co-founder & Managing Director of ClearView Research, an agency that specialises in research, strategy, and engagement projects focused on diverse and underrepresented communities. In 2022 he was featured in Forbes annual 30 under 30 list for Social Impact.

His debut book That Peckham Boy was recently published by Penguin Random House. Kenny is a trustee of several charities such as BBC Children in Need. Alongside Lord Michael Hastings CBE, he is also the co-founder of My Brothers Keeper, a voluntary group that supports men (mainly serving long-term sentences) in 6 prisons across the UK.

@Kenny.Imafidon

Michaela Jeffery-Morrison

Founder and MD, Women in Tech World Series

Michaela Jeffery-Morrison is Founder and Managing Director of the Women in Technology World Series, the worlds largest events series dedicated to women in technology. The series was founded in 2015 with one clear mission: to promote equality and advancement for the remarkable women and individuals within our global community.

Through a series of live events, The Women in Technology World Series, delivers network opportunities, inspirational talks, and knowledge to women and businesses within the technology sector. The series draws in hundreds of speakers from the world’s most innovative companies to their events in London, Amsterdam, Dublin, Texas, and Washington DC, and tens of thousands of attendees from across the globe.

Michaela has directed numerous high-profile conferences in both Europe and the US. By applying remarkably high standards to all aspects of event creation and management, she has built a powerful reputation, ultimately leading her to launch The Women in Tech World Series which was bought by Delinian in November 2023.

Mike Williams

Co-founder of Flake Blake

Mike Williams is the Co-founder of Flake Bake LTD, the largest producer of authentic handmade Jamaican patties in London. After completing his undergrad in Economics and Politics and an MSc in Business at Warwick University, Mike joined
forces with his father to establish their bakery in Peckham. Twelve years later, Flake Bake supplies over 20,000 handcrafted patties each week to local takeaways, restaurants and street food vendors.

Mikes hard work and dedication have propelled Flake Bake to win Channel 4s Aldi Next Best Thing 2024. Upon securing their contract with Aldi, Flake Bake sold over 100,000 of their famous Jamaican Beef patties in less than 10 days. Flake Bakes success has garnered widespread media attention, with features in publications such as The Grocer and The Independent, as well as coverage on ITV News, Channel 4 News, BBC London News and BBC Radio London.

@flakebakepatties

Patrick Adjaye

Creative Director for DCN, Founder & Editor in Chief at Dresscodenation

Patrick, the Creative Director at the renowned clothing brand DCN and Editor-in-Chief of the influential publication DressCodeNation, is a dynamic force in the world of fashion. With a flair for innovation and a keen eye for style, he spearheads the creation of cutting-edge fashion trends, establishing himself as a true trendsetter.

As a multifaceted leader, Patrick bridges the gap between fashion and niche audiences, effortlessly connecting with those who appreciate unique and authentic style. His expertise extends beyond fashion, as he excels in forging strategic connections between brands and talent, orchestrating impactful campaigns, and developing innovative marketing strategies. Patrick’s visionary approach seamlessly links brands with cultural tastemakers, creating a dynamic synergy that sets trends and drives fashion-forward conversations.

@uptown_p1

Rachael Anson

DJ, Presenter & Producer

Since launching her career in 2016 Rachael has since brought her vibe to stages internationally with memorable appearances at Wireless, #Merky Festival, Strawberries & Creem, Berlin Carnival and more. Whilst supporting national tours & events with Koffee, Burna Boy, Stormzy, Stylo G, Kranium and Shaggy; which included commanding huge crowds at O2 arena.

Quickly becoming a household DJ name mixing sounds of the homegrown UK scene, Dancehall, R&B and more; Rachael has begun to join the worlds of music, fashion & collaborating with global brands including BOSS, Nike, Puma, Clarks, Salomon, Wray & Nephew & more. Whilst also appearing on global platforms such as Apple Music, Boiler Room and Serato. As a passionate creative and curator she launched her lifestyle brand Vibes Cant Done.

Her works have earned her awards such as UEAs Best DJ and nominated for Guap Galas Best DJ Award twice as well as Young Music Boss.

@rachelanson

Sanmi Adegoke

CEO of Rehoboth Property Group

Sanmi Adegoke is the CEO of Rehoboth Property Group (RPG), an award-winning entrepreneur and leading UK real estate developer. He is also a philanthropist, global speaker and thought leader in the industry.

Sanmis legacy is about changing the narrative of ownership and giving back to the community. He has received numerous accolades for his work, including Real Estate Developer of the Year, Entrepreneur of the Year and Property Developer of the Year awards. In addition to these achievements, he has judged various industry awards.

‘Faith, Tribe and Legacy’ are the pillars he lives by – success comes from family and relationships built over the years with those he loves and cares for unconditionally.

@sanmi.adegoke

Sayce Holmes-Lewis

Founder and CEO, Mentivity

Sayce Holmes-Lewis is a visionary Community Entrepreneur, dedicated Football Coach, and Inspirational Mentor with 25 years of experience in mentoring, personal development, consultancy, community safety, and strategic partnerships. As the Founder and CEO of Mentivity and New Landings, Sayce has transformed countless lives in London.

Driven by a commitment to racial equity and social justice, Sayce secured over 1,250,000 in 2023 for Mentivity, showcasing his ability to attract significant financial support. His collaborations with Goldman Sachs, Spotify, Avanade, PPL, and Sky harness resources for impactful community empowerment.

Sayce’s influential network includes the Mayor of London, City Hall, and MOPAC, contributing to grassroots community development. As the youngest member on MOPACs London Policing Board, he pioneers systemic reforms for safer communities.

A champion for social value, Sayce is dedicated to dismantling systemic inequities, reshaping community development, and setting new standards for inclusive growth and social justice.

@sayceholmeslewis

Shaun Sookoo

Founder of Viomar Holdings and CEO of Food Division

Shaun is a dedicated professional, with a mission to create job opportunities that not only foster economic growth, but also make significant social impact. Currently with 9 directorships, over 120 jobs created and a combined group turnover in excess of 15 million within Food Manufacture, Care, Property, Logistics and Non-Profit sectors.
His non-profit work revolves around access to education supporting projects in Jamaica, Gambia and the West Midlands. Through his work, he strives to build a more inclusive and equitable professional landscape, developing and nurturing talent and producing spaces where everyone has a chance to succeed.

www.shaun-sookoo.com

Tiwalola Ogunlesi

Confidence Coach, Author, Founder – Confident and Killing It

Tiwalola Ogunlesi is a globally recognised coach specialising in positive psychology, international speaker, author and the founder of Confident and Killing It. Confident and Killing It is a purpose driven organisation and community that wakes women up to their worth so they can be confident, unstoppable and dare to live the life they truly desire. Through engaging and practical workshops, media and coaching programmes, Tiwalola equips women with the tools they need to overcome fear and self-doubt, programme their minds for success and live out their biggest dreams. Her first book Confident and Killing It was published by HarperCollins in July 2022, to great acclaim.

Since starting in 2018 she’s upskilled over 10,000 women across the world and delivered engaging experiences for brands and organisations such as Google, Meta, The Times, Deloitte, Morgan Stanley, Viacom CBS, Spotify, Squarespace, UN Women UK, The Oprah Winfrey Leadership Academy and many more.

@tiwalowla

Victoria Omobuwaj

CEO and Founder of Sunmo Health and Wellness

Victoria Omobuwajo, a visionary entrepreneur, is the driving force behind Sunmo, a brand that redefines health-conscious living with its innovative product range. From the invigorating Berry Burn to the revitalising Balance Green and the collagen-boosting Collagen Protein, Sunmo offers a diverse array of products that seamlessly blend health-conscious ingredients with delightful flavours.

With a remarkable journey showcasing her tenacity, creativity, and business acumen, Victoria has made a significant impact on both the retail and direct-to-consumer industries. Within just two years of its launch, Sunmo has secured coveted retail spaces in esteemed establishments such as Selfridges, Sainsbury’s, and Whole Foods Market.

Renowned chef Gordon Ramsay joined forces with Victoria as her business partner and investor. Investing 150,000 into the Sunmo business. His expertise and strategic guidance have propelled Sunmo to new heights, solidifying its position as an industry frontrunner.

As Sunmo continues to revolutionise the food industry, Victoria remains an inspiration.

@victoriaomobuwajo
