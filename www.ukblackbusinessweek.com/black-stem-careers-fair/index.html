Black STEM Careers Fair

Wednesday 15th October

10am – 2pm

Black STEM Careers Fair is a space where innovation meets opportunity and diversity fuels progress. This event connects young Black talent with industry leaders, technology innovators, and dynamic entrepreneurs who are reshaping the STEM landscape.

Through practical sessions, inspiring keynotes, and live speed mentoring, ambitious attendees can discover pathways to breakthrough into incredible careers and the essential skills for success. Whether youre at the start of your career journey or considering an early career pivot, this event will deliver the connections, insights and inspiration to help you become a future leader in STEM!

Embrace the spirit of innovation, collaboration, and inclusion at the Black STEM Careers Fair and connect with a community of brilliant young minds ready to excel in science, technology, engineering and maths careers.

Key themes:
1. Learn how to showcase your unique value to stand out in competitive job markets
2. Understand how to navigate the evolving landscape of new technologies to future-proof your career
3. Gain new connections to unlock exciting opportunities to jumpstart your career
4. Find out how to pitch your skills and experience in interviews to land roles at top companies
5. Discover how to build the foundational skills needed for early careers success

Speakers

Daniel Brommell

Director – Talent Acquisition – Europe, Middle East & APAC, WWT

Daniel has 25 years experience in the employment sector, developing skills within agency environments and the last 6 years in-house at WWT.

He has built successful recruitment teams from the ground up, across Permanent and Contract solutions within the international marketplace, covering Europe, Middle East, and APAC (India, Singapore, Australia, and Japan).

This has given Daniel an appreciation for different cultures, local legalities and approaches when recruiting, as well as ensuring a positive experience for candidates that require a human approach to managing them through the process.

Daniels leadership style is very much about building real relationships through honesty, empowerment, and humility. Trust being the key core value Daniel strives to create in all relationships he develops.

Dylan Aupetit

Senior Global Partner Manager & EMEA WWT For ALL Lead, WWT

Dylan is strategic partnerships leader with 10+ years experience driving revenue growth through global partner ecosystems across vendors and suppliers. He has a proven track record of developing joint GTM strategies, building scalable alliances, and aligning cross-functional teams to deliver measurable business outcomes.

Adept at influencing executive stakeholders and navigating complex, multi-regional environments. Dylan is passionate about inclusive leadership and cultural transformation, with expertise in Diversity, Inclusion & Belonging. He aims to inspire young people, regardless of background, to recognise the strength in their stories and believe in their ability to achieve great things.

Kwesi Bimpong

Co-Founder, Black Apprentice Network & Black Undergrad

Kwesi Bimpong is the Co-Founder of the Black Apprentice Network and Black Undergraduate, two of the largest diverse career communities in the UK, connecting over 5,500 members with the largest employers. Recognised in the Powerlist 2024 as the Number 1 Black Undergraduate in the UK, Kwesi has demonstrated leadership in creating opportunities for underrepresented talent and has worked with the Lord Mayor and the City of London Corporation on inclusion initiatives.

He recently completed a degree apprenticeship at Goldman Sachs, graduating with a First-Class degree in Computer Science. Since then, Kwesi has been recognised as one of the UKs most active builders on Lovable, using AI tools to develop software in the sectors he is most involved in, particularly the early careers space.

Rubin Poku

Co-Founder, Black Apprentice Network & Black Undergrad

Rubin Poku is the Co-Founder of the Black Apprentice Network (BAN), one of the UKs largest communities for current and aspiring apprentices, with over 5,000 members nationwide. Through BAN, he has developed partnerships with organisations including Deloitte, BT, and the National Trust to deliver programmes and events that open doors for Black talent in early careers.

Alongside this, Rubin works at Amazon as a Project Manager within the People Experience & Technology (PXT) team during his Chartered Manager Degree Apprenticeship, where he focuses on strategy, innovation, and organisational development. He previously gained experience at KPMG and also serves on the KFC Youth Advisory Board and the UCAS Student Advisory Board, helping to shape policies that improve access and opportunity for young people across the UK.

Elijah Olanipekun

Co-Founder, Black Apprentice Network & Black Undergrad

Elijah Olanipekun is the Co-Founder of the Black Apprentice Network and Black Undergrad, platforms dedicated to empowering ambitious Black students to break into the UKs top companies while supporting employers to recruit diverse, high-performing talent.

After graduating from his Digital and Technology Solutions apprenticeship, Elijah worked in Dublin as a High Growth Consultant at Salesforce, where he built expertise in digital solutions and client growth. He later returned to the UK to focus full-time on expanding BAN into one of the countrys most impactful early careers communities.

Elijahs mission is to bridge the gap between underrepresented talent and world-class employers, combining professional experience with a passion for equity and opportunity.

Chris Gorman

VP, Talent Acquisition, Block

Chris Gorman is Vice President of Talent Acquisition at Block, the global technology company behind Square, Cash App, Afterpay, and TIDAL. Over his 13 years with Block, he has led across a range of strategic areas, including Corporate Real Estate & Workplace and serving as Head of People for Blocks Cash App division.

Chris is passionate about building inclusive, high-performing cultures and is an active sponsor of emerging talent. He currently serves as executive sponsor for Blocks PRIDE community (LGBTQ+ affinity group), championing representation and equity across the organisation.

He also completed the UC Berkeley CHRO program co-led by Laszlo Bock, deepening his expertise in shaping people strategy at scale.

Rebecca Tomasiewicz

Transformation & Integration Leader, bp

Rebecca Tomasiewicz is a dynamic and versatile leader with a unique blend of skills in design, management accounting, and engineering. She holds a MEng in Aerospace Engineering and has built a career that spans across diverse industries and roles.

Currently leading team mobilisation and readiness in the Technology Transformation Office for bp Rebecca is focussed on putting people at the heart of the change. She previously served as the Experience Design leader for People Experience in Technology at bp.

Rebecca leads teams that put people at the heart of a problem, applying design thinking to improve end-to-end product experiences and drive organizational effectiveness. An empathetic leader, Rebecca is passionate about fostering inclusive work environments and has been involved in diversity initiatives throughout her career.

Bessie McDonald-Phelps

Senior Early Careers Advisor, EY

Bessie McDonald-Phelps is a Senior Advisor in EYs Early Careers team where she leads on Undergraduate and Graduate attraction efforts across the UK, driving inclusive outreach and engagement with early talent. She joined EY in 2022 as part of the Early Careers Operations team, focusing on Tax and Law. Prior to EY, Bessie held various recruitment roles within the legal market, gaining deep insight into competitive hiring landscapes and what it takes to stand out. Bessie aims to share practical advice and insider tips on navigating early careers recruitment processes and making a lasting impression.

Vaughn Gordon

Senior Early Careers Advisor, EY

Vaughn Gordon is a Senior Advisor in EYs Early Careers team, where he leads on their Summer Internship programme, helping to shape the future of early careers talent. He began his journey at EY in 2015 as a Recruitment Coordinator, quickly moving into the Early Careers team with a focus on Operations and a passion for driving diversity. With a decade of experience in talent acquisition and programme development, Vaughn brings deep insight into building inclusive pathways for emerging professionals. Vaughn is excited to connect with aspiring STEM students, offering guidance and sharing his experience navigating and thriving at EY in the Early Careers space.

Mary Kyei

Recruiter, Talent Acquisition, HSBC UK

Mary Kyei is an experienced Recruiter with 8 years in Talent Acquisition. She has recruited for talent across industries ranging from Not-for-profits to Banking. Mary is passionate about finding opportunities to hire for skills, helping organisations source talent, and supporting individuals in advancing their careers.

Santina Bunting

Civil Engineering Apprentice, Arup

Santina Bunting stands out as a trailblazer; the only one out of 150 students to embark on an apprenticeship journey after GCSEs. She completed her Level 3 Civil Engineering Apprenticeship at Arup in two years, earning a Distinction in her BTEC. Now a qualified Technician with the Institute of Civil Engineers, Santina is in her second year of a Level 6 Degree Apprenticeship at Warwick University whilst also working at Arup

Santina was recently the Youngest overall winner of the Greater Birmingham Chamber of Commerce Young Professional of the year 2024 in its 24-year history, as well as Apprentice of the Year winner.

Passionate about empowering apprentices, Santina has spoken at the House of Lords, the Annual Apprenticeship Conference, and has contributed to the UCAS Apprenticeship Advisory group and the government’s recent skills for life campaign.

Anthony Chandler

Assurance Senior Manager, EY

Anthony Chandler is a Senior Manager at EY in London, specialising in assurance and audit across the Technology, Media & Telecommunications (TMT) and Real Estate sectors. Since joining EY in 2013, he has worked in both the UK and internationally, including a year in Canada and other short-term global projects. Anthony has been ACA-qualified for nine years through ICAEW and holds a BSc (Hons) in Accounting for Management from Aston University. He served as an Investment Manager on secondment to the Social Business Trust, leads UK pro bono initiatives within EY, and is Trustee and Treasurer at Southwark Law Centre. Known for his analytical depth and collaborative leadership, Anthony brings expertise in IFRS, financial reporting, and strategic advisory, with a strong commitment to innovation, governance, and social impact.

Jhan Gibson

Program Manager, Global Workforce Development, Salesforce

Jhan Gibson has been a Programme Manager at Salesforce for the past 3 years and has held many roles in the Environmental Social Governance (ESG) profession. From working in nonprofits, to the legal sector and now tech, Jhan has a wealth of experience managing programmes and nurturing the next generation of talent.

Shane Franklin

Program Manager EMEA, Salesforce

Drawing on over a decade in recruitment and learning & development, Shane is a Program Manager at Salesforce, where he leads early career and talent development programs in the tech sector. He has supported diverse talent in navigating pathways into corporate careers and progressing into leadership roles. Passionate about learning and development, he is committed to building impactful programs that empower people to grow, thrive, and succeed in their careers.
