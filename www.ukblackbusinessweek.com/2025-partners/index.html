Black Business Magazine is the UKs leading publication dedicated to celebrating, supporting, and spotlighting Black entrepreneurs, professionals, and changemakers. As a platform rooted in empowerment and representation, the magazine shares inspiring stories, expert insights, and practical strategies to help readers grow thriving businesses and impactful careers. With each quarterly issue, we showcase excellence across sectors from tech and finance to fashion, wellness, and the arts, while addressing the systemic barriers faced by Black communities. More than just a magazine, were a movement creating space for visibility, connection, and legacy-building. As proud media partners, were honoured to amplify voices and ideas shaping the future. Join us in rewriting the narrative, one story at a time.

Partner profile

Black Apprentice Network is all about connecting diverse, underrepresented talent with top employers through apprenticeship and early-career opportunities. Started by Elijah, Kwesi and Rubin, who are either current ot former apprentices, who are really into increasing Black African and Caribbean representation in the workforce, BAN has grown into a thriving community of over 5,000 members. The network provides essential resources like mentorship, resume workshops, and interview prep to help candidates navigate the competitive job market.

BAN partners with leading early talent teams at large UK apprenticeship employers such as Accenture, Norton Rose Fulbright, and BT, working to help them create diverse pipelines by providing access to a highly motivated and diverse pool of talent within their community. Through targeted events, webinars, and skill-building initiatives, BAN equips young professionals with the tools to succeed. The organization is a vital platform for aspiring apprentices and early-career professionals, committed to breaking down barriers and empowering underrepresented groups in their pursuit of career success.

blackapprenticenetwork.co.uk

Proudly independent, private sector-led, and not backed by the government, Tech London Advocates fundamentally believes that London is one of the worlds leading tech hubs. Our mission is to inspire, support and connect the tech sphere to allow more black women to excel and tech companies to perform better through diversity and inclusion.

Partner profile

Legally Diverse, a platform where I provide advice and mentorship to aspiring solicitors hoping to enter the profession. In the past two years I have worked with several organisations to further my goal of making the legal profession more accessible and diverse. These organisations include: * LawCareers.Net; * All About Law; * Vantage; * Little Law; and * the Commercial Law Academy. In October 2023, I launched a brand new resource for aspiring lawyers called the Legally Diverse Networking Cards. The deck of cards feature 92 questions for aspiring lawyers to ask those in the profession when attending law firms and/or legal networking events. The cards have been colour coded to indicate the best questions to ask people in the recruitment teams, trainees, associates and partners. The aim of the cards is to ensure that aspiring solicitors leave these events feeling as though they have learnt enough valuable information to better their application forms, interviews and understanding of the profession in practice.

legallydiverse.com
