Black Media, Culture & Sports Talks

Thursday 16th October

6pm – 9.30pm

The Black Media, Culture & Sport Talks celebrate the transformative power of Black voices in shaping media landscapes and cultural narratives. This event brings together trailblazing entrepreneurs, innovative creatives, and sporting legends who are redefining what it means to lead in their industries.

Through compelling conversations and practical panel discussions, attendees will learn how to harness their platforms for positive impact, navigate industry challenges, and create opportunities to elevate key conversations. Whether youre an aspiring media professional, a creative entrepreneur, or a sports industry executive, this event showcases the possibilities when talent, passion, and purpose align.

Join this event to connect with a community of innovators who know that media, culture, and sport are powerful vehicles for storytelling, representation, and social change.

Key themes:
1. Transform media landscapes by championing Black narratives and setting new industry standards for representation
2. Understand how to use different platforms to amplify your voice and kickstart much-needed conversations
3. Master the art of storytelling to build authentic connections with audiences while driving social impact
4. Navigate the intersection of music, sport, and media to create multi-dimensional career opportunities
5. Change the game by stepping into pivotal leadership roles that influence wider industry direction

Speakers

Hamid Ibrahim

Co-Founder & CEO, Kugali Media

Hamid Ibrahim is a three-time Emmy-nominated creative executive, award-winning production designer, and visionary entrepreneur shaping the future of African storytelling. He is the Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer of Kugali Media, a pioneering visual entertainment company redefining African narratives through animation, comics, and immersive media.

Hamids creative journey spans some of Hollywoods most iconic productions, including The Lion King (2019), Dumbo (2019), The Predator (2018), The Darkest Minds (2018), and Dolittle (2020). His most groundbreaking achievement is Iwaju, a first-of-its-kind African sci-fi series co-created with Walt Disney Animation Studios , marking the first external studio collaboration in Disneys 100-year history. As co-creator and production designer, Hamid helped shape a story that earned three Emmy nominations and international acclaim. As an international speaker, Hamid regularly shares insights on creative leadership, innovation, and the business of storytelling.

Sheneice Charway

Music Executive & Podcast Host

Sheniece Charway is a leading figure in the British music industry, known for her work as the Head of Black Music & Culture, EMEA, at YouTube. Since joining in 2019, she has championed Black talent and culture, playing a key role in the growth of artists both on the platform and throughout the UK. Charway is the driving force behind the YouTube Musics Legacy Series, which celebrates Black culture and raises awareness of Black issues through music and art. Her influence has earned her multiple accolades, including features on the Powerlist.

Prior to YouTube, she worked at Columbia Records, where she spearheaded a first-of-its-kind Facebook game for Rick Ross. Charway also hosts her own faith-based podcast, Battles & Blessings, which shares the stories of entrepreneurs and creatives.

Luke Henry

Audio Producer, talkSPORT

Luke Henry is an aspiring Sports Presenter, having undertaken a variety of roles in his time with talkSPORT. From assistant producing live shows, working across live football, and producing content for big games such as the Premier League, Champions League and International tournaments.

Luke has a deep passion for football and is lucky enough to voice his opinions on his beloved Arsenal FC for both talkSPORT and, more recently, Sky Sports, being part of the Fanalysis app, a new way and platform to discuss football.

Luke is also an active supporter of the Raheem Sterling Foundation, recently producing a podcast series to document the experiences of a group of young adults from the Young Brent Foundation on a ten-day trip to Jamaica.

Mya Graham

Audio/Visual Content Producer, talkSPORT

Mya Graham is an Assistant Producer at the Wireless Group, where she contributes to the dynamic world of live sports radio. A proud UCFB graduate, Mya’s journey is a testament to her dedication and talent, having quickly progressed from an apprentice to producing flagship shows at talkSPORT. In her role, she focuses on crafting compelling content and emphasises the importance of networking within the industry. Mya transformed her passion for football into her dream job right after university, offering a unique perspective on the art of sports broadcasting and the realities of the media landscape.

Darren Lewis

Assistant Editor, Columnist & Football Writer, Daily Mirror

Darren Lewis is the Assistant Editor at the Daily Mirror and a seasoned commentator on talkSPORT and CNN. With extensive experience in sports journalism, he provides valuable insights into the evolution of the industry. Throughout his career, Darren has navigated the challenges of maintaining journalistic integrity in a fast-paced media environment, emphasising the importance of building and sustaining key relationships within the sports world. His journey from a local news reporter to the President of the Sports Journalists’ Association highlights his commitment to the field and his dedication to fostering the growth of sports journalism.

Ade Oladipo

Sports Reporter, Broadcaster, Content Creator

Ade Oladipo is a prominent figure in the sports broadcasting industry, serving as the lead presenter for DAZN Boxing and making a significant impact on the talkSPORT network. His career trajectory includes his groundbreaking role as the first Black presenter for an Amazon Prime Premier League game, showcasing his dedication and talent in sports analysis. Ade exemplifies modern career progression with his ability to build a personal brand, navigate multiple sports, and thrive as a content creator through his successful YouTube channel. His journey is a testament to hard work, versatility, and innovation in the evolving landscape of sports media.

Kweku Afari

Freelance Broadcaster, Presenter & Radio Producer

Kweku Afari is a rising television star and producer at talkSPORT, known for his innovative approach to career development in the digital age. With a diverse portfolio that includes collaborations with prominent organisations such as Sky, ESPN, and DAZN, Kweku brings a wealth of experience to the table. As one half of the dynamic RSN podcast, he offers a fresh perspective and an unfettered voice on key industry issues. A dedicated patron of the Raheem Sterling Foundation, Kweku is passionate about mentorship and is committed to creating pathways for the next generation of talent. His emphasis on adaptability and a multi-skilled approach makes him a leader in the ever-evolving media landscape.

Toni Afoke

Freelance Broadcast & Producer

Toni Afoke is a talented freelance broadcaster and producer, recognised for his contributions to prominent platforms such as talkSPORT and Her Football Hub. With a keen eye for insightful football analysis, he has successfully established himself in the sports media landscape. As one half of the RSN podcast, Toni brings an unfiltered perspective to the world of sports conversation.

Beyond his media work, Toni is passionate about social impact, actively collaborating with the BCOMS and the Raheem Sterling Foundation to highlight the significance of leveraging one’s platform for the benefit of disadvantaged youth in sports journalism. Through his experiences, he aspires to inspire the next generation of media professionals, encouraging them to pursue their dreams in the industry.

Joyce Havemeier

Senior Marketing Manager, UK Black Business Week & Show

Joyce Havemeier is a sports, events and cultural marketing specialist, currently leading marketing at UK Black Business Week. Focused on brand marketing and digital strategy, with a strong track record in membership and subscription models.

At Arsenal FC, she led the club’s adult membership proposition and was the clubs first Womens Network Co-Chair as part of the club’s EDI leadership team.

A passionate advocate for EDI in sport and business, Joyce is also a host, moderator and voice-over artist.

Lisa Maffia

Queen of UK Garage & Founder, Maffia Rum, House of Maffia & Lyrical UK

Lisa Maffia is the Queen of UK Garage. She is a multi-talented singer-songwriter, musician, fashion designer, model, and presenter.

She began her career as the only female member of the award-winning collective So Solid Crew in 1999. Throughout their time at the top, So Solid notched up five Top 20 Hits including the platinum-selling single 21 Seconds which appeared on the just as popular album: They Dont Know (3x Platinum).

A legend in her own right, she has accumulated various awards along the years including Mobo, Brit, and Q. She consistently tours the UK, Europe, and the world, captivating thousands of fans with her performances.

Lisa is also a multifaceted entrepreneur. She founded Maffia Rum, an aged spiced rum brand, the House of Maffia, an interior design and staging company, and Lyrical UK, a hub for musicians, brands and businesses to connect.

Shannie Mears

Co-Founder & Head of Culture & Communities, The Elephant Room

Shannie is the Co-Founder & Head of Culture & Communities at The Elephant Room, a creative advertising agency based in London. Having worked with Global brands such as; PUMA, Chivas Regal, Barclays, Pinterest, and more. Shannie has become a cultural leader in the creative space after 10 years of empowering, creating, and building a more purposeful industry. She was named Management Today’s 35 under 35 young woman in Business, She was also named Forbes 30 under 30, BBC 1xtra Future figure, and sits on the committee for Soho house CWH. Shannie lectured for 2 years at Kingston University within the creative and cultural industries department, and previously sat on the Race disparity advisory board at No.10 Downing street advising on race and policy.

Honey Akinbo

Brand Marketing Manager, Barclays

Honey is an experienced Brand and Social Media Marketing professional, specialising in developing creative campaigns that bring sport and culture together. She has led groundbreaking social strategies for Barclays sponsorship of the Premier League and Barclays Womens Super League, creating content that drives fan engagement, amplifies key moments, and sparks conversation across digital platforms.

In her role as Head of Barclays Football Social Media, Honey has delivered high-impact campaigns that connect authentically with diverse audiences, shaping how the brand shows up in the fast-moving digital space. She is passionate about storytelling that inspires the next generation of fans, particularly at the intersection of football, fashion, and lifestyle.

Alongside her marketing career, Honey is also an established sports presenter, known for her engaging analysis and fresh perspective on football, culture, and lifestyle.

Dr Tru Powell

Multi-Award Winning Entrepreneur & Personal Branding Expert

Dr. Tru Powell is a multi-award winning personal brand expert and creative entrepreneur. Tru has over 15 years in enterprise, the arts, and live events, and is very passionate about supporting the next generation of entrepreneurs to get visible and build their personal brands. As well as the Founder and Director of Tru Powell Consultancy & Events Ltd, Tru is the founder of the Inspire Awards. Previously, he served as the CEO of the Multicultural Business & Community Champion Awards (MBCCs). He is also the CEO of the Black Business Magazine, a premier online and print publication dedicated to uplifting and empowering the Black community, and Director of Aston Performing Arts Academy. Alongside being a successful entrepreneur, Tru is very passionate about equality and often speaks publicly about what it means to be anti-racist.

Oyin Akiyini

Global Brand Director, Chivas Brothers

Oyin is curious, entrepreneurial and experienced in building brands that place culture and purpose at the heart of their consumers. With over 15 years in the industry working with brands such as Nike, Boiler room, Youtube, Apple Music and now leading the culture department for Chivas Regal. Oyin has made sure that engaging her audiences is reflected in everything she does. Oyins extensive understanding of youth culture has led her to working with some of the best taste makers across industries likes; fashion, music and drinks/alcohol space.
